Portfolio Refinement — Full Improvement Plan

shibam-portfolio-omega.vercel.app  ·  Principal Designer Review  ·  Vercel / Linear / Stripe / Apple lens  ·  June 2026

01 CTA Hierarchy Critical ›
Issue · 4 competing CTAs in the hero alone

The hero contains: "VIEW CASE STUDIES" (black button), "START A PROJECT →" (ghost link), and the nav has "START A PROJECT" (bordered button). The visitor's eye has no hierarchy to follow. Two actions equal zero decisions.

Now — 3 competing actions
VIEW CASE STUDIES  ·  START A PROJECT →  ·  START A PROJECT (nav)
After — clear hierarchy
VIEW MY WORK (primary black button)  ·  Let's Talk → (nav, secondary only)
Fix · Hero CTA rule

One primary action per section. In the hero, that action is showing the work — not starting a project. The visitor hasn't seen anything yet. "Start a project" at this moment is asking for commitment before delivering proof. Remove the ghost "START A PROJECT →" link from the hero entirely. The nav CTA handles hire intent. The hero CTA handles discovery.

Fix · Section 6 (Availability) has weak CTA

"CHECK AVAILABILITY" is fine in concept but the section copy — "New projects are taken on a limited basis, by enquiry" — creates friction before the action. It reads like a waitlist, not a welcome. The CTA should invite, not gate.

Now
New projects are taken on a limited basis, by enquiry. CHECK AVAILABILITY
After
Currently taking on new projects. Let's talk about yours. START A CONVERSATION →
Fix · Process section CTA is buried

"SEE THE FULL PROCESS →" is a valid CTA but the section copy around it ("AI-accelerated iteration, hand-finished detail") undercuts the craft message you're selling. Fix the copy first, the CTA will land better.

02 Copywriting Critical ›
Issue · Hero subline buries the lead and contains a trust-killer

"built fast with an AI-accelerated workflow, finished by hand" — "AI-accelerated" in a premium client's subline is a red flag. Premium clients hear: "a robot built part of this." The strongest claim ("finished by hand") is buried after an em-dash at the end of a three-part sentence.

Now
Premium, design-led websites for restaurants, boutiques, and brands that refuse to look ordinary online — built fast with an AI-accelerated workflow, finished by hand.
After
Custom websites for restaurants, boutiques, and independent brands — designed from first principles, delivered in weeks, not months.
Issue · Features strip uses industry clichés

"Design-Led", "Built to Convert", "AI-Accelerated", "Production-Ready" — every Framer agency and Webflow freelancer uses 3 of these 4 phrases. "AI-Accelerated" actively harms your premium positioning. "Production-Ready" is the minimum bar for any professional, not a differentiator.

Now
01 Design-Led · 02 Built to Convert · 03 AI-Accelerated · 04 Production-Ready
After
01 Business-first · 02 Converts browsers · 03 Delivered in weeks · 04 Maintained for years
Issue · Stats section makes weak claims

3 business categories, 0 templates, 100% responsive. "0 templates" is a negative phrased as a positive. "100% responsive" is table stakes since 2012. "3 categories" is a thin number that limits rather than impresses. These are numbers a junior developer would list.

Now
3 — categories · 0 — templates used · 100% — responsive
After (use real numbers from your 11 projects)
11 — projects shipped · 3 — industries · 100 — Lighthouse score, every build
Issue · Process step 02 copy contradicts the brand message

Step 02 of your process says "AI-accelerated iteration, hand-finished detail." This is the third time "AI-accelerated" appears on the homepage (subline, features strip, process). Repetition of a phrase you're also trying to downplay makes it feel defensive rather than confident.

Now
Design & Build — AI-accelerated iteration, hand-finished detail.
After
Design & Build — Fast iteration, precise execution. Every decision justified before it's shipped.
Issue · About section has a semantic contradiction

The "Why This Exists" section (section 01) says: "Most businesses don't need a website. They need a reason to be remembered." That's a strong opener. Then the About section (section 02) opens with: "Good design isn't decoration. It's the difference between a business that gets remembered and one that gets scrolled past." The word "remembered" appears in both consecutive sections. This doubles the trick and halves the impact of each.

Section 02 opener — now
Good design isn't decoration. It's the difference between a business that gets remembered and one that gets scrolled past.
Section 02 opener — after
Good design isn't decoration. It's the moment a stranger decides whether to trust a business with their money.
Issue · Case study "quote" has no attribution

Bong Pizza: "The food had more personality than the website did. That gap was the whole brief." — No name, no role, no source. Without attribution this reads as copy you wrote yourself. Unattributed quotes are worse than no quotes — they feel fabricated. Same issue for Peyala Café and Monolith Architecture.

Now
"The food had more personality than the website did. That gap was the whole brief."
After (with real attribution OR reframe as design brief)
The brief, in one sentence: make the website as confident as the food. — OR — reframe as: "The brief was simple: close the gap between how good the food was and how ordinary the website looked."
Issue · "00" section number is architecturally nonsensical

Your sections run 00 → 01 → 02 → 03 → 04 → 05. Section "00" implies a prelude or introduction, but the hero is actually your primary thesis statement, not a prologue. More importantly, using "00" signals that you copied a convention (editorial magazine numbering) without questioning whether it applies. Your numbering starts at 01 everywhere else — the hero eyebrow should either be removed entirely, or use a different system (just the role label, no number).

Now
00 — FRONTEND DEVELOPER / DIGITAL EXPERIENCE DESIGNER
After
FRONTEND DEVELOPER · DIGITAL EXPERIENCE DESIGNER (no number — the hero earns this without a chapter marker)
03 Typography High Impact ›
Issue · Crimson italic used 3 times — the trick is spent by use #2

The crimson italic serif appears as: 1) customers in the hero H1, 2) remembered in the About section pullquote, 3) the entire philosophy pullquote ("A website is the first conversation…") is styled in the same face. Three instances of the same typographic signature collapses the technique from "intentional accent" into "template." The hero instance is the strongest — keep only that one.

Fix: Remove the crimson italic from the About pullquote. Set that line in the regular serif italic, or in the body sans. Reserve crimson for hero only. The philosophy pullquote stands on its own weight as a full-block serif italic — it doesn't need the crimson to land.

Issue · H1 line-height is tighter than the text (76px height for 80px font)

The hero H1 is rendering at 80.28px font-size / 76.27px line-height. A line-height ratio of 0.95 means consecutive lines would collide. This works only because the headline wraps naturally with enough descender clearance, but it's a timing bomb at certain breakpoints and viewport widths. Line-height on display type should be 0.95–1.05 intentionally if it's a one-liner, or 1.1–1.2 if it's multi-line. Audit this value at every breakpoint.

Issue · H2 "About Shibam Pandab" renders at 16px / weight 400

This heading is visually lighter and smaller than the body paragraphs below it (18px). An H2 that's visually smaller than its body content inverts the information hierarchy. Either this is a screen-reader-only heading that should be visually hidden, or it needs to be promoted to an eyebrow label (11px, tracked, uppercase) and a real H2 display should be the first visible text of the section.

Issue · "AI-ASSISTED WORKFLOW" heading in the philosophy section

This H3 exists inside a section titled "DESIGN PHILOSOPHY." The section has 3 philosophy principles (01 Clarity, 02 Typography, 03 Outcome) — then adds a 4th called "AI-ASSISTED WORKFLOW" which is a process disclosure, not a philosophy. The category mismatch creates a structural inconsistency. Either move this to the Process section or reframe it as a philosophy: "Tools serve the work, not the other way around."

What's working · Body typography

18px body / 29.25px line-height (1.625 ratio) is solid — slightly generous but appropriate for this editorial register. The serif/sans pairing is coherent. Don't change the type system itself, only these specific problem instances.

04 Project Presentation Critical ›
Issue · Every project image slot says "VISUAL PENDING"

This is the most damaging thing on the page. You're applying for senior roles and pitching to international clients — and the work section has no work visible. A hiring manager or premium client closes the tab the moment they see "VISUAL PENDING" repeated 3 times. You have 11 deployed projects. Three of them need real screenshots in the next 48 hours before anything else in this plan matters.

Priority order for screenshots: MyPortfolio → NovaCare Clinics → Noir Étoile. These three represent your strongest technical range. For each: take a full-page scroll screenshot at 1440px width, crop to show the hero + one section below, export at 2x resolution. Frame them in a simple browser chrome mockup if available.

Issue · The project section intro says "Three businesses" but shows 3+ projects

Section 02 intro copy: "Three businesses. Three different problems." But the section then shows at least Bong Pizza, Peyala Café, and Monolith Architecture — and you have 11 total projects. Either commit to showing 3 best-in-class and making that framing accurate, or change the copy to reflect the actual scope.

Now (misleading)
Three businesses. Three different problems. The same approach: understand the business before touching the design.
After (accurate)
Selected work. Each one a different business problem, the same approach: understand before you design.
Fix · Both "VIEW CASE STUDY →" and "VIEW LIVE →" appear on each project

Two CTAs per project card creates the same hierarchy problem as the hero. Which action matters more? If the case study is the full story, lead with that. "VIEW LIVE" is secondary — it's proof, not the narrative. Stack them with clear visual weight difference: case study as primary, live site as a small text link below.

Fix · Project numbering jumps from 01 → 04 → 05

In the work section, project numbers jump from 01 (Bong Pizza) to 04 (Peyala Café) to 05 (Monolith). This implies there are projects 02 and 03 that are hidden or missing, which creates confusion. Either renumber sequentially starting at 01 for the shown work, or remove numbers entirely from project cards (they don't need them — the section already has a section number).

Fix · Case studies need outcomes, not just problems

Current structure: Problem → Quote → Context → CTAs. Missing: Solution and Outcome. The most credible portfolio entries end with a measurable result or a specific design decision that solved the brief. Even one sentence works: "The redesign replaced a buried PDF menu with an inline, filterable card layout — the business reported an increase in direct phone orders within the first week."

05 Spacing & Visual Rhythm High Impact ›
Issue · Hero section padding-top is 80px but padding-bottom is 0px

The hero section has pt-20 (80px top) and no bottom padding. The visual weight sits at the top, which means the hero content feels like it floats against the features strip below it without a resolved bottom. Add at minimum pb-16 (64px) to let the CTAs breathe before the border-bottom divider.

Issue · "SCROLL" rotated label on the features strip

The rotated vertical "SCROLL" text on the right edge of the 4-column features strip is pure decoration. The visitor is already scrolling. This is a tic borrowed from agency sites where it appeared in full-height splash sections with no visible content below the fold. In your context — where the next section is already partially visible — it's meaningless noise. Remove it entirely.

Issue · Philosophy section has two pullquotes in sequence

Section 02 (About) contains: a large pullquote ("A website is the first conversation...") AND a separate full-screen text block in section 04 ("A website is never the product..."). Two full-width editorial text statements back to back with similar weight dilutes both. The second one ("A website is never the product. It's the proof that the business behind it pays attention.") is actually the stronger line — consider elevating it and removing or demoting the first.

What's working · Section separation via 2px borders

The border-b-2 border-ink system for section dividers is clean and editorial. It creates structure without using background colors or heavy visual weight. Keep this — it's one of the strongest systemic decisions on the page.

06 Premium Details & Micro-interactions Polish ›
Fix · Footer email is unlinked plain text

shibampandab@gmail.com in the footer renders as plain text — clicking does nothing. On mobile it's invisible as an action. This is a broken conversion path on a client-acquisition portfolio. Wrap in <a href="mailto:shibampandab@gmail.com"> and add a subtle underline-on-hover. Also: consider a custom domain email (hi@shibam.dev or similar) — Gmail signals student project to international clients paying premium rates.

Fix · Hover states on nav links go to red — the wrong signal

Nav links have hover:text-red. Crimson red on hover implies "danger" or "destructive action" in conventional UI. In this editorial context it's a brand color, but on nav links it creates a micro-moment of visual tension. The hover state should feel like refinement, not alarm. Consider hover:text-ink from a muted starting color, or an underline reveal, rather than a color shift to the accent.

Fix · Link-style CTAs use generic arrow →

Every ghost/text CTA ends with " →". This is fine — directional arrows in text links are a clear convention. But the arrow is a static character. At this quality level, the arrow should animate on hover: slide 4px right, then return. One line of CSS: transition: transform 0.2s ease; group-hover:translate-x-1. You already have group on these elements — activate it.

Fix · Image placeholder transition when real images load

Once you add real project images, wrap each in a container with overflow: hidden and apply a subtle scale reveal on scroll: scale(1.03) → scale(1.0) over 600ms with an ease-out curve. This makes images feel like they're "arriving" rather than just appearing. Do not add parallax — it cheapens the editorial register.

Fix · Page entrance animation

The current page load begins with a blank cream screen for ~1.5 seconds before content appears. This is the Framer Motion / React hydration delay. Whatever the technical cause: the blank screen must be filled. Either add a minimal loading state (the wordmark fading in alone), or use SSR to render above-fold content without JS dependency. A premium portfolio never greets you with an empty cream rectangle.

07 Conversion Journey Audit Critical ›
Journey map — where trust is lost

Landing (Hero) ✓ Strong. Headline commands attention. Subline loses with "AI-accelerated." Two CTAs split attention.

↓ Trust (Why / About) ✓ Copy quality is good. The "HOW I THINK" section is genuinely strong. However the repeated "remembered" across sections 01 and 02 dulls the message.

↓ Projects ✗ Complete trust failure. "VISUAL PENDING" × 3. No outcomes. Unattributed quotes. Project numbering inconsistency (01 → 04 → 05). This is where the visitor decides to contact you or leave — and it currently makes them leave.

↓ Process ↗ Reasonably good. Three steps, clear flow. "AI-accelerated" in step 02 is the only copy problem here.

↓ Availability ✓ The scarcity signal ("limited basis, by enquiry") works, but the CTA "CHECK AVAILABILITY" is cold. "START A CONVERSATION" is warmer and converts better.

↓ Contact / CTA ✓ "Have a business worth building for?" is a strong closer. Don't change it. The CTA "START A PROJECT" works here because the visitor has now seen your process and philosophy.

↓ Footer ✗ Email unlinked. No LinkedIn, GitHub, or social proof. Footer is the last impression — currently leaves the visitor with no secondary path.

Fix · Add one trust anchor in the footer

Add a single, quiet link to your GitHub profile in the footer. For a frontend hiring context, GitHub is your second portfolio. Don't add a full social bar — one link is enough. For client contexts, a LinkedIn link works similarly. The footer currently has: logo, nav links, email. Add: one line below the email, lighter color — "See the code → github.com/ShibamPandab"

09 Remaining Issues (Not in Scope Yet) Future ›
No custom domain email. shibampandab@gmail.com is fine for personal use. For a premium freelance business targeting international clients at £3,000–£8,000+ per project, a custom domain email is table stakes. hi@shibampandab.com or studio@shibam.dev would cost <₹500/year.
No real testimonials with attribution. Once you have 1–2 clients who've seen results, get a one-sentence quote with their name and business. This single addition would be more credible than any design change on this page.
Individual project pages are not audited. The /projects, /services, /process, /contact routes have not been reviewed here. They each need the same copy and CTA audit.
Mobile experience not reviewed. The initial screenshots were taken at 788px width. At that size the 4-column features strip likely stacks — that transition needs to be verified. The hero H1 at 80px on mobile likely wraps awkwardly.
No Open Graph image. When this URL is shared on LinkedIn or WhatsApp, it likely shows a blank preview. An OG image (1200×630) with the wordmark and tagline on the cream background takes 30 minutes to set up and makes every share look professional.
Contact page / form not reviewed. The conversion endpoint — where interested clients land after clicking "START A PROJECT" — has not been audited here. It needs the same trust-signal attention as the homepage.
